Analytical Gap Statement — SAS-219 Positioning
Where WinterStorm2030 sits in the SAS-219 Analytical Landscape
STRATEGIC FORESIGHT
What futures are possible?
Horizon scanning, expert elicitation, futures narrative development. Qualitative and exploratory. Tells us where we might be going — but cannot tell us whether institutions will be capable of governing when we get there.
OPERATIONAL WARGAMING
How would forces respond?
Force posture, kinetic and sub-kinetic scenario design, escalation dynamics. Operationally rigorous. Tells us how military assets would be deployed — but assumes institutions coordinating that response are functionally capable.

Panel Gap Addressed: The inaugural SAS-219 session identified an open question: "How do we write down success criteria, and how do we meet them?" Auracelle WinterStorm2030 is spearheading this workstream. The framework below proposes five approaches to success criteria design, five generic SAS-219 KPIs, and five WinterStorm2030-specific measurable KPIs — with a live demo showing what each produces.
Five Approaches to Defining Wargame Success Criteria
01 / BACKWARDS-ENGINEERED TANGIBLES
Define success by identifying exactly what decision-makers need from the wargame output, then design criteria that confirm those outputs were delivered. Inverts typical practice — grounds success in decision-maker utility, not activity metrics.
02 / GOVERNANCE OBSERVABLES
Define success as achieving specific, observable governance behaviors during scenario execution — binary, scenario-linked, directly tied to wargame design intent. E.g. "Iceland classifies cable event as governance attack within 6 hours."
03 / ACTOR IMPROVEMENT (g-GWC Δ)
Define success as measurable improvement in governance capacity scores across wargame cycles — particularly for CD and ASI domains where baseline scores are lowest. Tracked by Auracelle WinterStorm2030 across the 2026–2028 arc.
04 / RQ INSIGHT QUALITY
Define success not just as Research Question coverage, but as the novelty and actionability of insights generated per RQ. Not "did we address RQ3" but "did RQ3 analysis produce an insight that changes how we think about procurement strategy?"
05 / INSTITUTIONAL ADOPTION RATE
Define success by whether wargame outputs are adopted in real institutional processes — exercises, doctrine reviews, capability planning, policy documents. The ultimate downstream criterion. Requires long-term tracking beyond the wargame cycle.

Svalbard Treaty (1920)
INTEGRATED
Grants Norway sovereignty over Svalbard while giving signatory states equal rights to engage in commercial activity. Creates a permanent legal gray zone: Norway governs, but 46 states hold access rights — including Russia. Article 9 demilitarisation clause creates governance ambiguity in an era of dual-use infrastructure and autonomous systems deployment.
GOVERNANCE SEAM
Norwegian sovereignty vs. Russian access rights — Article 3 economic rights create permanent legal ambiguity adversaries exploit for below-threshold presence operations.
GRAY ZONE CONDITION
Article 9 demilitarization applies to Norway but not Russia's Barentsburg presence. Dual-use infrastructure (research stations, satellite ground stations) operates in governance ambiguity.

NATO Washington Treaty — Article 5 Collective Defence
INTEGRATED
Article 5 collective defence commitment — an attack on one is an attack on all. The governance gray zone lies in the threshold: "armed attack" is undefined, and hybrid operations are specifically engineered to remain below it. The critical governance question is not whether Article 5 applies, but whether allied institutions can agree it applies fast enough to matter.
GOVERNANCE SEAM
Article 4 consultation threshold vs. Article 5 activation — the governance window between "incident" and "armed attack" is the primary gray zone adversaries exploit in the High North.
GRAY ZONE CONDITION
Below-threshold hybrid operations (cable cuts, GPS denial, cognitive operations) are designed to remain in the Art. 4/5 governance gap — where consultation is triggered but collective defence is not.
SCENARIO INJECT GENERATED
Simultaneous below-threshold incidents across 3 Nordic states — does the aggregate cross Art. 5? NDM governance decision window: 6 hours before adversarial tempo advantage is decisive.
